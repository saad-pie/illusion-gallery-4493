Lines

Müller-Lyer Illusion

Two straight lines of equal length appear different due to the direction of the arrowheads at their ends. Which line looks longer?

Patterns

Café Wall Illusion

Parallel lines appear to slant due to the staggered arrangement of alternating dark and light tiles. Notice how the horizontal lines seem to bend.

Shapes

Rubin Vase

Do you see a vase, or two faces in profile looking at each other? This classic figure-ground illusion demonstrates how our perception can alternate between two distinct images.

Kanizsa Triangle

Your brain perceives a white, equilateral triangle in the center, even though it's not explicitly drawn. It's formed by the arrangement of three "pac-man" shapes and three angled lines.

Impossible Objects

Impossible Penrose Stairs

This structure appears to be a staircase that ascends or descends infinitely, an endless loop that defies the laws of three-dimensional geometry. While a perfect 3D representation is complex in pure CSS, observe how the lines suggest an impossible continuity.
